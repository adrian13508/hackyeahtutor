SQ3R Method Summary:

The SQ3R method is an effective study and reading technique developed to improve understanding and retention of material. It stands for Survey, Question, Read, Recite, and Review.

S - Survey: Begin by quickly surveying the material. Look over headings, subheadings, summaries, and any bolded or highlighted text to get an overview of the content.

Q - Question: Turn headings into questions. Asking questions before you start reading helps to focus your attention and sets a purpose for reading.

R - Read: Read actively, searching for answers to the questions you've formed. Focus on understanding key concepts and ideas rather than reading word for word.

R - Recite: After reading, close the book and try to recite, in your own words, the key points and answers to your questions. This helps solidify the information in your memory.

R - Review: Review the material after a few days to reinforce what you've learned. Revisit the questions and see if you can still answer them without looking at the text.

The SQ3R method not only helps in improving comprehension but also in making studying more efficient by focusing on active reading and engagement with the material.
